Linear recurrence by generalized prefix sum

@October 24, 2024

I recently read papers on modernized recurrent neural networks (RNNs), such as minLSTM/GRU and Mamba. One insight I gained is that RNNs with linear recurrence can be parallelized using a generalized version of the classic prefix sum algorithm. This simple yet powerful generalization is crucial for scaling RNNs to much larger sizes.

This generalization was studied in Blelloch (1996). The paper introduced some less common concepts, such as semiassociative operators and companion operators. This note offers an alternative formulation based on the notion of semigroup action, which may be more succinct. Towards the end, we address this question: given a recurrent affine transformation ht:=Wtht−1+bth_t := W_t h_{t-1} + b_tht​:=Wt​ht−1​+bt​﻿ with h0=0h_0 = 0h0​=0﻿ and Wt,btW_t,b_tWt​,bt​﻿ constant, how can we compute {ht}t=1…T\{ h_t \}_{t=1\dots T}{ht​}t=1…T​﻿ efficiently?

With this generalization, we now have much more possibilities than just summing real numbers. In particular, square matrix with multiplication form a semigroup since matrix multiplication is associative (but not commutative).

Prefix sum on a sequence of actions
